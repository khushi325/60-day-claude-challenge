# 🚚 Day 30 – Supply Chain Optimizer with Claude AI

## 📌 Overview

On **Day 30** of the **60 Days Claude AI Challenge**, I built and explored an interactive **Supply Chain Optimizer** using Claude AI.

This simulator demonstrates how strategic decisions across procurement, manufacturing, warehousing, transportation, and inventory management influence overall business performance. Throughout the simulation, each decision immediately updates operational metrics, allowing users to understand the trade-offs involved in supply chain optimization.

The challenge provided hands-on experience with supply chain strategy while showcasing how AI can simplify complex business concepts through interactive simulations.

---

# 🎯 Objective

- Understand the fundamentals of supply chain optimization.
- Learn how different business decisions affect overall performance.
- Explore supplier, manufacturing, warehousing, transportation, and inventory strategies.
- Analyze business metrics in real time.
- Improve decision-making using data-driven insights.

---

# 🚚 About the Simulator

The Supply Chain Optimizer is an interactive business simulation that allows users to design an optimized supply chain for a randomly generated company.

The simulator includes:

- Beginner-friendly introduction
- Random company profiles
- Supplier strategy selection
- Factory location planning
- Warehouse strategy optimization
- Transportation method selection
- Inventory strategy planning
- Live business metric updates
- Final optimization dashboard
- Overall Supply Chain Score
- Strengths, weaknesses, risks, and recommendations

---

# 🔄 Simulation Workflow

The simulation follows these stages:

1. Read the Introduction
2. Review Company Profile
3. Choose Supplier Strategy
4. Select Factory Location
5. Choose Warehouse Strategy
6. Select Transportation Method
7. Choose Inventory Strategy
8. Monitor Real-Time Business Metrics
9. Review Final Optimization Dashboard
10. Analyze Overall Supply Chain Score
11. Study Recommendations
12. Replay with a New Company

Every supply chain decision involves trade-offs. Optimizing one area, such as cost or speed, may impact inventory levels, customer satisfaction, or operational risk. Achieving balance is essential for long-term business success.
