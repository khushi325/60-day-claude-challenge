# 🏥 Day 26 – Prior Authorization Workflow Simulator with Claude AI

## 📌 Overview

On **Day 26** of the **60 Days Claude AI Challenge**, I built and explored an interactive **Prior Authorization (PA) Workflow Simulator** using Claude AI.

The simulator recreates the real healthcare prior authorization process by allowing users to move a patient case through the workflow from **Patient → Provider → Payer**. It includes drag-and-drop interactions, document collection, payer review, approval/denial decisions, appeals, and educational explanations at every stage.

This challenge helped me understand how healthcare workflows operate while demonstrating how AI can be used to create engaging educational simulations.

# 🏥 About the Simulator

The Prior Authorization Workflow Simulator is an interactive educational application that teaches how insurance authorization requests move through the healthcare system.

The application includes:

- Multiple patient scenarios
- Drag-and-drop workflow
- Interactive document collection
- Educational explanations
- Progress tracker
- Workflow dashboard
- Approval, Pend, Denial & Appeal paths
- Workflow summary

# 🧑‍⚕️ Patient Scenarios

The simulator provides four realistic healthcare scenarios:

### 🦵 Elective Surgery (Knee Replacement)

- Patient requires total knee replacement.
- Requires imaging reports and medical necessity documentation.

---

### 🧲 MRI (Lower Back)

- Patient experiences persistent lower back pain.
- Requires conservative treatment documentation before approval.

---

### 💊 Specialty Medication

- Biologic therapy for Rheumatoid Arthritis.
- Requires laboratory reports, diagnosis, and prior therapy documentation.

---

### 🏨 Inpatient Admission

- Severe pneumonia requiring hospital admission.
- Requires admission orders and insurance verification.

---

# 🔄 Workflow Process

The simulated workflow follows these stages:

1. Patient identifies healthcare need.
2. Patient visits provider.
3. Medical necessity evaluation.
4. Collect required clinical documents.
5. Submit Prior Authorization request.
6. Payer review.
7. Approval / Pend / Denial.
8. Appeal or Peer-to-Peer review (if needed).
9. Final determination.
10. Workflow summary.

---

# 📂 Required Documents

Examples of required documents include:

- Clinical Notes
- Letter of Medical Necessity
- Imaging Reports
- Laboratory Results
- Physician Orders
- Prior Treatment History
- Insurance Verification

Submitting incorrect or incomplete documentation reduces workflow efficiency and may lead to delays or denials.

# 🧠 Key Learning Points

- Prior Authorization ensures medical necessity before treatment approval.
- Complete documentation improves approval rates.
- Missing documentation may result in a pending request.
- Denials can often be resolved through appeals or peer-to-peer review.
- Efficient workflow management reduces treatment delays.

---

# 💡 Biggest Learning

Healthcare workflows involve coordination between patients, providers, and insurance payers. Proper documentation and communication are critical to achieving timely approvals.
