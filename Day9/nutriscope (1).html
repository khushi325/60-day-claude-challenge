      Educational Disclaimer

      NutriScope is an educational self-tracking tool. It provides general, approximate estimates of energy and nutrient intake based on typical food composition values and standard formulas. It is not a medical device, not a diagnostic tool, and not a substitute for advice from a qualified doctor, registered dietitian, or other healthcare professional.

      Individual nutrient needs vary with health conditions, medications, pregnancy, lactation, growth stage, and lab-confirmed deficiencies — none of which this app accounts for. Always confirm significant dietary changes, supplementation, or concerns about deficiency or excess with a qualified professional.

      Nutrition Data Sources

      Per-100g nutrient values used in the food database are approximate figures, generalized from public nutrition composition references and rounded for common cooked or edible portions:

      • Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT 2017), ICMR – National Institute of Nutrition
      • USDA FoodData Central, U.S. Department of Agriculture

      Actual nutrient content varies with brand, variety, ripeness, cooking method, and regional recipe differences. Treat all figures as estimates for planning purposes rather than lab-verified values.

      How Targets Are Calculated

      Calorie targets use the Mifflin-St Jeor basal metabolic rate equation, scaled by a standard activity multiplier (Sedentary to Very Active). Macronutrient targets follow common protein-per-kg and fat-percentage-of-calorie conventions, with carbohydrates filling the remainder.

      Micronutrient targets are simplified, generalized adult RDAs broadly aligned with ICMR-NIN 2020 guidance, split by gender where relevant. They are not personalized for clinical conditions. Sodium is tracked as an upper limit rather than a target to reach.

      About Risk Analysis

      The Risk Analysis tab applies simple, transparent threshold rules to today's logged totals (for example, flagging iron intake under 50% of target as a possible anemia-risk signal). These are illustrative educational heuristics only, built for awareness — not clinical risk scores.
